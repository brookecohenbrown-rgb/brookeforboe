About

My name is Brooke Brown and I am running for a seat on the PPCSD Board of Education because I am a parent, a neighbor, and a person who never stands idly by when I know I can make a positive impact.

Before moving to the area I worked for 15 years as a professional costume designer, on and off Broadway and in many theaters around the country. This career taught me about managing large budgets, leading teams of people with competing priorities, and delivering results under intense pressure. Today I bring the same skills to every challenge that I take on.

My daughter starts Pre-K this upcoming fall and this district’s future is personal to me. Last November I attended a Future Ready Forum where the superintendent laid out the need to right size the schools. What I heard alarmed me — a major decision driven without adequate data, without a clear plan, and without genuine community input. When the Superintendent recommended closing Cold Spring Early Learning Center shortly after, I knew I had to act.

So I authored a petition calling for the delay in school closures until the board and administration had developed a fully researched and published plan, taking into account the impacts on both students and communities. Nearly 350 people signed it, almost as many as people who voted in the previous year’s budget vote. I helped to rally over 75 neighbors who showed up together to a board meeting and made their voices heard. We forced the board to listen — they delayed their vote and formed the Building Utilization Advisory Committee which I now serve on.

What I have witnessed over the past 5 months has confirmed everything I feared. We have a board that is unwilling to listen openly, ask the hard questions, or take action when the community’s needs are not met. We have an administration that resists sharing information or doing the work necessary to dig us out of the hole we are in. Our district is at a real crossroads — financially, academically, and structurally. We need board members who will show up and do the work, and I am ready to be one of them.

I've Done the Research

I have spent countless hours researching our districts, other districts, and the particulars of education in NYS. I attended a Prospective Board Members workshop at BOCES and understand the responsibilities and commitment I'm signing up for.

I'll Show Up

I have been to every BOE meeting since November of last year, including various committee meetings, and now attend all meetings of the Building Utilization Advisory Committee. My attendance record is better than most current board members.

I'll Listen

Since getting involved I have had many interactions with community members, board members, and teachers from this and other districts. I invite dialogue and varying opinions, something the current board has proven to work against.

I'll Tell You the Truth

My most effective way of reaching people has been through the facts. When working against the Cold Spring vote I provided all the data and research I had access to and put it in front of people so they could make their own decisions on the matter.

Values

Accountability

I will demand answers and forward-thinking from an institution that currently sits in silence and refuses to act. Every decision should withstand a community member's most basic question: why?

Transparency

I will share information about our schools that the administration has previously withheld or obscured. Easily accessible budgets and data for every hard-working tax payer.

Community

I believe schools belong to the people. I'll fight for decisions that reflect what our community wants and deserves — not what's convenient for the institution.

What I'll Push For

  • →Holding the administration accountable for every dollar spent on behalf of all taxpayers.
  • →Pushing for real, long-term financial and operational planning — not short-term fixes that kick hard decisions down the road.
  • →Making district presentations, budgets, and documents easily accessible to every community member, not just those who know where to look.
  • →Fostering genuine dialogue at board meetings where public voices are heard, respected, and actually responded to.
  • →Reaching our communities where they are — through meaningful outreach that goes beyond a notice in the newspaper.
  • →Refusing to accept mediocrity — our community deserves a district that holds itself to the highest possible standard.

What I'll Stand Against

  • →Band-aid solutions that paper over deep problems instead of solving them.
  • →Spending priorities that are disconnected from what's actually happening in our classrooms.
  • →Treating concerned parents and community members as nuisances rather than the partners they are.
  • →Accepting vague non-answers from the administration on questions that deserve real responses.
  • →Lamenting declining enrollment without taking any meaningful action to reverse it.
  • →Expecting taxpayers to keep writing checks without giving them transparency, accountability, or results in return.
